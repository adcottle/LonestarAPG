Private Tour, Texas State Library and Archives at
Lorenzo de Zavala State Archives and Library Building
1201 Brazos Street, Autin, TX 78701

The Texas State Library and Archives Commission (TSLAC) is hosting a private tour of the newly renovated Lorenzo de Zavala State Archives and Library Building in Austin.

The TSLAC tour starts at 10:30 a.m. and consists of:
  • a brief introduction to the collections in the Learning Center at TSLAC,
  • a tour of Reference, with a focus on new equipment,
  • a tour of the Genealogy print collection,
  • a visit to the Archives Reading Room, including a view of the Conservation Lab, and
  • a visit to the 2nd stack area, for that special "behind the scenes" glamour.

After a break for lunch we will meet at 1:30 p.m. at the Texas General Land Office (GLO)
1700 N. Congress Avenue
Austin, Texas 78701-1495
512-463-5001
Toll Free: 800-998-4GLO (4456)

We will have a tour of the Map Vault, archival vault and scan lab, see some of the most interesting documents and maps, and learn about the genealogical resources available at the GLO.

Parking is available across the street in the Bob Bullock Texas State History Museum at 1800 N. Congress Avenue ($8 for a full day, $1 per hour). See map at http://www.thestoryoftexas.com/special/pdf/tshmbusmap.pdf. The Bob Bullock Texas State History Museum is shown at the upper left with an arrow marking the parking garage entrance. The General Land Office is just south of museum in the building marked "SFA."

Driving directions from I-35 are: take exit 235A (15th Street / MLK Blvd. / State Capitol); turn west on Martin Luther King Jr. Blvd.; turn left (south) on N. Congress Avenue; turn right (west) onto 18th Street. The Museum's underground parking garage entrance will be on your right. The museum is open from 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. if you want something to do before or after the chapter meeting.

At 3:00 p.m. we will finish up with a brief business meeting.

Please contact President Robert Meaux if you would plan to attend.

Description Date Time Location

1st
Meeting
Saturday, 17 March 2012
10:00 a.m. - 12:00

NARA open until 4:00 p.m. for research
Chapter meeting and presentation on:
  • using the new NARA-Southwest Branch facilities and records, and
  • the latest on the 1940 U.S. Census to be released on 2 April 2012
National Archives, Southwest Region
2600 West 7th Street, Suite 162, Fort Worth, TX, 76107
phone: 817-831-5620

The entrance to the archives is on the east side of the shopping center. Look for the red awning over the door.

This is the facility with microfilm records and computer access. There is no access on Saturday to the textual (paper) records which are held at a different location.

There is access to some digital records that are not available on the public internet, including the institutional version of Ancestry.com which covers records not covered by the library version. Microfilm records are available and the archivists have some fabulous finding aids they use in-house that are not published online. This is a great chance to get individual help from those who know the archive records best while also having access to a large group of knowledgeable researchers willing to offer advice. Check out the NARA-SW website at http://www.archives.gov/southwest/ to learn more about the records available.

The 7th Street facility is in the shopping mall that used to be the Montgomery Ward Plaza. There are lots of shops and several restaurants in the immediate area: Pei Wei, Starbucks, Chick-Fil-a, a deli, a grill and pub, and a couple of more formal restaurants. After our meeting many of us will have lunch as a group or multiple groups at different restaurants. Or you can work in the archives which is open until 4:00 p.m. The research room holds 10-15 people so we may have to take turns at lunch and research if everyone wants to research after the meeting.

Please contact Debbie Parker Wayne if you would like to attend. Seating is limited to 25.
